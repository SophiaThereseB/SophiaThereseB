Biography

Vincent van Gogh was born on March 30, 1853 in Groot-Zundert, Holland. His father was a pastor, so he believed his calling was to become one as well. However, it took him years to realize that he would become an artist. As a young boy, he was highly insecure and struggled with controlling his emotions. This continued as he grew older. Eventually, he was sent to an asylum for treatment for his madness. Although he was different from most of us, van Gogh's legacy lives on in the art world and he's deemed one of the most influential artists to this day.

His Art

Vincent van Gogh was a post-impressionist artist. Post-impressionism is an art movement that spanned from around 1886 to 1905. The movement came about as a reaction against Impressionism and the natural perception of light and color. Van Gogh's art was either based on his struggle against insanity or his understanding of the essence of man and nature.
